The Challenge

Healthcare Challenges in India

Despite progress, India's healthcare system faces inefficiencies that hinder care delivery for ~1.46 billion citizens, with public health spending at only 1.6-1.8% of GDP.

Limited Record Access

About 60% of healthcare facilities rely on paper records, and while the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission enables digital access, only ~30% of private facilities are integrated, creating information gaps.

Limited AI Support

AI tools for reading or summarizing lab tests and reports are used in only 10-15% of hospitals, mostly urban private ones, leading to inefficient data processing.

Time-Consuming Reviews

Manual record reviews consume 20-30% of consultation time when patients return after weeks, reducing time for patient care.

Limited Decision Support

Junior doctors lack access to Clinical Decision Support Systems, though pilots via Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission are underway, risking inconsistent diagnoses.

Manual Report Analysis

About 70% of diagnostic processes are manual, with automation limited to urban private hospitals, increasing the burden on healthcare professionals.

Physical File Dependency

Many patients carry physical files, with 60% of facilities using paper records; losing files risks losing medical history, disrupting care continuity.

The Cost of Inefficiency

Healthcare Inefficiencies

  • Doctors spend 30-50% of their time on paperwork rather than patient care
  • 20-30% of diagnostic tests are repeated due to lost results or inaccessible records
  • Manual record-keeping leads to a 10-20% error rate in patient information
  • Rural patients travel 3+ hours on average to access care and records

An Opportunity for Transformation

With over 67 crore Ayushman Bharat Health Accounts and 42 crore linked records, digital solutions like ABDM offer a chance to revolutionize healthcare delivery in India.
